## The Big Idea

Kalima is the NYT Games for the Arabic-speaking world.

Not a translation. Not a clone with Arabic text bolted on. A platform built from the ground up for Arabic — its language, its culture, its speakers. Every game, every word, every interaction designed specifically for someone whose first language is Arabic and who has never had a daily word game that felt made for them.

**The insight:** 400 million Arabic speakers. Zero credible daily word game platforms. NYT Games is worth billions. The window is open right now.

## The Platform Vision

### One Home. Many Games.
`kalima.app` (or `kalima.com`) is the destination. A clean dark home screen showing all of today's games — which you've played, which you haven't, your streaks. Like opening a newspaper but it's games.

### Shared Identity Across Games
Every game feels like Kalima. Same font (IBM Plex Arabic), same dark palette, same tile language, same calm voice. A user who knows حروف will immediately feel at home in روابط.

### The Daily Ritual Loop
- Wake up → check Kalima
- Play حروف (5 min)
- Play روابط (3 min)
- Share results → friends see it → they open Kalima
- Come back tomorrow

This is not a game. It's a habit.

### Social Without Social Features
No friends lists. No DMs. No comments. Just the shareable result card — an Arabic RTL tile grid that looks unlike any Western share card. When someone shares it on WhatsApp or Twitter, it signals: "I play Kalima." That IS the social layer.

## Why Now

### The Arabic Puzzle Vacuum
Wordle launched in 2021. Arabic clones appeared briefly but died. No one has built a serious, well-designed, consistently maintained Arabic word games platform. The space is empty.

### Ramadan 2027 is the Distribution Window
Ramadan is the highest-engagement media moment in the Arab world. People stay up late, share content obsessively, and build new daily habits during the month. A platform with real users by January 2027 can explode during Ramadan 2027 (Feb 17 – Mar 18).

Timeline to get there:
- **Now – June 2026:** Launch حروف, build initial audience
- **July – Oct 2026:** Launch روابط, grow to 10K DAU
- **Nov 2026 – Jan 2027:** Polish, Arabic word curation, retention loops
- **Feb 2027:** Ramadan push — 50K DAU target
- **Post-Ramadan:** Monetize, expand

### The Acquisition Angle
At 50K+ DAU with strong retention, Kalima becomes an acquisition target for Arabic media companies (MBC, Shahid, Arab News Group, Rotana). They want daily-habit digital products and don't know how to build them. We do.

## The Moat

### 1. Arabic Language Curation
Building a great Arabic word game requires deep language work:
- A curated word list (not just a dictionary dump)
- Handling Arabic morphology (roots, forms, diacritics)
- RTL layout that actually works at every breakpoint
- Words that feel fair — not obscure, not trivial

This takes time and taste. It's not something you can outsource or auto-generate. Every word on every day is a editorial decision. That's the moat.

### 2. Cultural Fit
The brand, the voice, the humor — these are calibrated for Arabic speakers, not translated. A word that's fun in English might be awkward in Arabic. A category in Connections needs to map to shared cultural knowledge (movies, food, sports, history). Kalima has to feel like it was built by someone who grew up speaking Arabic.

### 3. First-Mover Brand
Once users have a streak, they don't leave. The first Arabic word games platform to hit critical mass (10K+ daily habitual users) will be very hard to displace. The brand becomes the category.

## Brand in One Page

**Name:** كلمة (Kalima) — "word" in Arabic. Simple. Says exactly what it is.

**Logo:** The letter ك inside a rounded square tile. Works at any size. Instantly communicates the game mechanic.

**Color:** Kalima Purple `#6B35C8` — no major Arabic app owns this color. We do now.

**Font:** IBM Plex Arabic — geometric, screen-optimized, modern.

**Voice:** Informal MSA. Concise. Proud.
- Win: **كلمة واحدة. كافية.**
- Streak: **أسبوع كامل. أصبحت عادة.**
- Push: **كلمة اليوم تنتظرك.**

**Share card:** Dark background, RTL tile grid, Eastern Arabic numerals. Recognizable in any Arabic social feed.

**What it is NOT:** An educational app. A children's game. A translation. A calligraphic tourist product.

## The North Star Metric

**Weekly Active Puzzlers:** Users who complete at least 3 puzzles in a 7-day window.

This is the one number that matters. Not downloads. Not registered users. Not sessions. People who came back multiple times this week to play.

At 10,000 WAP, Kalima has a real business.
At 50,000 WAP, Kalima has an acquisition story.

## What Makes This Win

NYT Games succeeded not because they had the best technology or the biggest marketing budget. They succeeded because:

1. **The games were genuinely good** — fair, satisfying, daily
2. **The brand felt trustworthy** — you knew the puzzle was curated by someone who cared
3. **Sharing was social currency** — posting your Wordle grid meant something
4. **Habit compounds** — once you have a streak, you protect it

Kalima wins by doing all four of these things, in Arabic, for Arabic speakers who have never had this.

That's the whole play.
